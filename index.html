About Clicklah

Mid May 2016,

Noah always had a strong urge to journal events that he experience throughout his career in Technology and his hobby in Photography .

Noah then approached Lucas Tan who had 8 years experience in Photography.

After a brief conversation over coffee, Noah shared his proposal officially  via email subjected "Clicklah Project”.



After exchanging thoughts for the goal and  mission.

It was as confirmed  - journal the events of experience based on skills and knowledge from sources we learn and courses we attended in photography.

Items and  products we have bought  for photography , software we have tried and  technology that we learn which we curate and filter which are meaningful for readers of clicklah

"The problem about sharing at social media like facebook , you will eventually forget or when you try to find the the things  you share, it's not as easy with blogging system that is more organized  via chronological dates and search post faster ."

About Noah

On Technology


Noah had a plethora of experience in technology over a span of 15 years, his domain focus is web development & internet security.

He has been featured in a local online magazine in 2007 for "youth hackers in MIRC"















He has later featured on the front page on a local newspaper in 2010 as an Internet Entrepreneur when he started the idea of a buy-and-sell portal. He has gain recognition as being  born gifted in IT savvy breaking the stigma in the Malay society.

The Change in Career

In 2012,  one of Noah close friend said:" You should come work here , it is nothing like you can imagine, come for an interview" .

Noah managed to secure a role after the interview and was leading a team from different walks of life for 5 years in an unnamed IT industry .

Due to the commitment and dedication at work Noah had when he started the job . He eventuallty halted what he has been doing as an internet prodigy



Passion revived.

Currently, he has left his current job to focus on what he was doing previously - his passion.


Because IT is always changing. I cannot simply keep paying for courses to pay and pay for the rest of my life .If you have passion, there should not be any problem learning from libraries or bibliography



He took advantage of everything he learns from his past working experiences from leadership roles to ideas he devised and created which inspires him to work on his current personal project still in progress

Lucas

It all started when he held his first family-owned Fujifilm compact camera that was running on film at the age of 10. The anticipation of what was going to be revealed after the pictures were developed was the same as one unwrapping a present for Christmas. Back then, film and developing of photos were considered a luxury in his family.

Fast forward to Secondary School when he joined the AVA club and got to handle his first Digital Cam which uses floppy disks as storage 'cards'. He had to carry chuck loads of floppy diskettes to cover events. The good thing was pictures can be viewed on the computer instantly after the activity was over.

Lucas then got his first DSLR (Nikon D70s) during his army life for 2 years which he traded for a Pentax K7 and 5 years later he has his Sony A7ii, guess "7" is his lucky number.

"I believe every picture is freezing a frame of life and immortalized a moment."

Joining WRS as a Docent from the span of 5 years since 2009 granted him access to places which public was not allowed.
He enjoys taking wildlife and animals and it was a perfect opportunity when he got to visit the zoo every now and then.

"People who are not rich are more generous and had more to give"

As inspired by Noah, Lucas was opened up to street photography when he took pictures of strangers off the street, with much more confidence this time.

The most important thing about anything is to be open-minded so you can see a different light in the same world.
